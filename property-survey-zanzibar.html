Our Process

Our Property Survey Process

01

Initial Consultation

We discuss your property transaction details, review preliminary documents, and understand your specific concerns and requirements.

02

Document Review

Our team reviews title deeds, survey plans, and land registry records to verify ownership chains and identify any documented encumbrances.

03

Field Survey

Licensed surveyors visit the property to verify boundaries, take precise measurements, assess physical conditions, and identify any encroachments or discrepancies.

04

Registry Verification

We cross-reference field data with official land registry records, zoning maps, and local authority documentation to ensure complete accuracy.

05

Risk Assessment

We identify and evaluate any risks including boundary disputes, zoning restrictions, access issues, or environmental constraints that may affect your investment.

06

Comprehensive Report

You receive a detailed property survey report including findings, risk assessment, recommendations, and all supporting documentation for your property transaction.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a property survey in Zanzibar?

A property survey in Zanzibar is a comprehensive assessment of a property's boundaries, physical condition, legal status, and development potential. It typically includes boundary verification, topographic mapping, structural assessment, and due diligence checks to protect buyers and investors.

Why do I need a property survey before buying land in Zanzibar?

A property survey protects your investment by verifying the property's actual boundaries, confirming the seller's ownership rights, identifying any encumbrances or disputes, and ensuring the land can be legally developed. Zanzibar's unique land tenure system makes professional due diligence essential for all property transactions.

How long does a property survey take in Zanzibar?

The timeline varies based on property size and complexity. A standard residential property survey typically takes 3-5 days for fieldwork, with an additional 5-10 days for report preparation and documentation review. Large commercial properties or those with complex ownership histories may take 2-3 weeks.

What does a property survey include?

Our property surveys include boundary verification, topographic mapping, physical site inspection, ownership documentation review, encumbrance checks, zoning verification, and a comprehensive due diligence report with recommendations. Additional services like structural assessments or environmental checks can be added.

How much does a property survey cost in Zanzibar?

Property survey costs in Zanzibar vary based on the property size, complexity, and scope of services required. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your specific property transaction or investment needs.

Can you survey foreign-owned property in Zanzibar?

Yes, we provide property survey services for both local and foreign investors. We understand the special regulations governing foreign land ownership in Zanzibar and ensure all surveys comply with the relevant land laws, including the Land Zoning Act and Investment Protection Act.

What is property due diligence in Zanzibar?

Property due diligence in Zanzibar is a thorough investigation of a property's legal and physical status before purchase. This includes verifying ownership records at the Land Registry, checking for liens or encumbrances, confirming zoning compliance, and assessing development potential. It is essential for protecting your investment.

Do you provide property surveys for commercial real estate?

Yes, we specialize in commercial property surveys including hotels, resorts, retail spaces, and mixed-use developments. Our surveys cover boundary verification, site assessment, zoning analysis, and development feasibility to support commercial property transactions and investments.

What are the common property issues found in Zanzibar?

Common issues include boundary disputes, unregistered structures, encroachments, unclear ownership chains, zoning violations, and incomplete land documentation. Our surveys identify these issues early, allowing buyers to make informed decisions or negotiate appropriate protections.
