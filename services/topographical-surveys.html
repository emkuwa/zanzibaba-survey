Our Service

Professional Topographical Surveys

Topographical surveys provide detailed information about the natural and man-made features of a piece of land. Our comprehensive surveys capture elevation data, contours, existing structures, boundaries, and all relevant terrain features.

Using state-of-the-art GNSS receivers, total stations, and drone technology, we deliver accurate topographical maps that form the foundation for successful project planning, design, and construction.

Our Process

A systematic approach to deliver accurate topographical survey results.

Survey Deliverables

Comprehensive documentation of your site conditions.

  • Detailed topographical map (AutoCAD & PDF)
  • Contour map at specified intervals
  • Coordinate data (CSV/DWG format)
  • Elevation report
  • Feature survey report
  • Digital terrain model (DTM)
  • Existing structures mapping
  • Boundary identification
  • Utility locations (where visible)
  • Vegetation survey
  • Drainage patterns
  • Site photography

Frequently Asked Questions

Survey duration depends on site size and complexity. A standard residential plot (500-2000 sqm) typically takes 1-2 days for field work. Larger commercial or infrastructure projects may take 1-2 weeks. Processing and delivery usually takes 3-5 working days after field work completion.
Our surveys achieve centimeter-level accuracy using GNSS and total station technology. Typical accuracy is ±1-2cm horizontal and ±2-3cm vertical. For drone surveys, we achieve ±3-5cm accuracy depending on ground control point density.
Standard contour intervals are 0.5m, 1m, or 2m depending on terrain complexity and project requirements. For detailed engineering projects, we can provide 0.25m or even 0.1m contours where the terrain allows.
Yes, we use drone photogrammetry as a complementary method to traditional survey techniques. Drones are particularly effective for large sites, providing rapid data collection and comprehensive coverage. We combine drone data with ground survey control for maximum accuracy.
