What is Construction Setting Out?

A complete guide to understanding how buildings are positioned on site with precision

Construction Setting Out

Transferring building plans from paper to the real world

What is Construction Setting Out?

Construction setting out is the process of transferring a building design from architectural and engineering drawings onto the actual ground. It involves measuring and marking the precise positions of walls, columns, foundations, roads, and other structural elements so that construction crews know exactly where to build.

Setting out is the critical bridge between the design office and the construction site. Without accurate setting out, even the best-designed building will be incorrectly positioned, potentially leading to structural failures, boundary encroachments, costly rework, and legal disputes. It is one of the first and most important steps on any construction site.

Why Setting Out Matters

The consequences of poor or inaccurate setting out can be severe:

  • Structural Integrity: Buildings rely on precisely aligned foundations and columns. Even small deviations can cause uneven load distribution, cracking, and structural weakness.
  • Legal Compliance: Buildings must be set back the required distances from property boundaries. Incorrect positioning can violate planning approvals and result in fines or demolition orders.
  • Cost Overruns: Errors discovered during construction require demolition and rebuilding, wasting materials, labor, and time.
  • Neighbor Relations: Encroaching on a neighboring property or obstructing their views and access can lead to expensive legal disputes.
  • Service Connections: Utilities such as water, sewer, and electricity must connect at the correct locations. Wrong positioning delays these connections and adds cost.

Types of Setting Out

Foundation Setting Out

This is the first stage of setting out on any building project. The surveyor marks the exact positions of footings, pile caps, and foundation walls. Pegs, nails, and string lines are used to define edges and corners. Ground levels are also established to ensure foundations are dug to the correct depth.

Structural Setting Out

As the building rises, the surveyor sets out column positions, floor levels, and wall lines for each storey. This ensures the structure remains plumb and true as it grows upward. In multi-storey buildings, setting out errors compound with each floor, making precision essential from the start.

External Works Setting Out

Beyond the building itself, setting out covers roads, parking areas, drainage, fences, landscaping features, and utility connections. These elements must align with both the building and the surrounding infrastructure.

As-Built Survey

After construction, an as-built survey verifies that what was built matches the approved drawings. This survey documents the actual positions and levels of the completed structure, which is essential for handover, future renovations, and property registration.

Methods and Instruments

Total Station

The total station is the primary instrument for construction setting out. It measures angles and distances simultaneously, allowing the surveyor to position points with millimeter accuracy. Modern total stations can store design coordinates and guide the operator to each setting-out point electronically.

GPS/GNSS

For large-scale projects such as roads, subdivisions, and infrastructure, GPS receivers provide rapid positioning over wide areas. Real-Time Kinematic (RTK) GPS achieves centimeter-level accuracy in the field, making it ideal for establishing control networks and setting out large elements.

Auto Levels

Auto levels are used to transfer and establish height levels across a site. They are essential for setting foundation depths, floor levels, and drainage gradients. The simplicity and reliability of auto levels make them a staple on every construction site.

Laser Levels

Laser levels project a visible or invisible beam that establishes a constant height reference. They are particularly useful for checking floor levels, installing suspended ceilings, and aligning fixtures and fittings.

Traditional Methods

String lines, timber profiles, and chalk lines remain useful for simple building projects. These low-cost methods work well for single-storey structures and domestic construction where high-precision instruments may not be justified.

The Setting Out Process

A typical setting out procedure follows these steps:

  1. Study the Drawings: The surveyor reviews all architectural and structural drawings to understand the building geometry, dimensions, and levels.
  2. Establish Control: Permanent survey marks are set up around the site, tied to national coordinates or a local grid. These serve as reference points for all measurements.
  3. Set Out Axes: The main building axes or grid lines are established first. These primary lines define the building's orientation and position on site.
  4. Mark Key Points: Individual foundation positions, column centers, and wall lines are marked using pegs, nails, or paint.
  5. Set Levels: Reduced levels for foundations, floors, and finished surfaces are established and marked on profile boards or stakes.
  6. Check and Verify: All measurements are checked independently. Diagonal measurements verify that rectangles are truly square.
  7. Hand Over: The setting out is clearly marked and communicated to the construction team, with written records provided.

Setting Out in Zanzibar

Zanzibar's construction sector is experiencing rapid growth, driven by tourism development, infrastructure investment, and a growing residential market. This expansion demands professional setting out services that understand local conditions:

  • Coralline limestone foundations: Zanzibar's rocky subsurface requires careful survey to plan foundation excavation accurately.
  • Coastal setbacks: Buildings near the shore must comply with government-mandated setback distances, requiring precise boundary and position surveys.
  • Historic Stone Town: Construction in the UNESCO World Heritage Site demands extreme precision to protect adjacent heritage structures.
  • Flood-prone areas: Accurate level setting is critical in low-lying areas to ensure finished floor levels provide adequate flood protection.

When to Hire a Professional Surveyor

While simple structures can sometimes be set out by experienced builders, professional surveyors should be engaged for:

  • Any project requiring planning approval
  • Multi-storey or complex buildings
  • Projects on challenging terrain or tight boundaries
  • Work near existing structures or utilities
  • Projects requiring high accuracy and traceable records

Professional surveyors bring not only precision instruments but also the expertise to interpret drawings, resolve conflicts between design disciplines, and adapt to site conditions. Their input prevents costly mistakes and ensures your project proceeds smoothly.

Cost of Construction Setting Out

The cost of setting out services depends on the project size and complexity. For a typical residential building in Zanzibar, setting out may cost between TZS 200,000 and TZS 800,000. Commercial and industrial projects command higher fees but represent a tiny fraction of the total construction budget. The cost of getting setting out wrong — rework, delays, legal issues — far exceeds the investment in professional services.
