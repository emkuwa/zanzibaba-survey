Professional Cadastral Survey Services in Zanzibar

A cadastral survey is the legal foundation of property ownership in Zanzibar. At Zanzibaba Survey & Geospatial, we provide comprehensive cadastral survey services that establish, document, and certify accurate property boundaries for official registration and title deed purposes.

Our licensed surveyors work in accordance with the Zanzibar Land Survey Act and Ministry of Lands requirements to create legally recognized boundary documentation. Whether you're registering a new property, resolving a boundary dispute, or subdividing land, our cadastral surveys provide the official documentation you need.

Our Process

Our Cadastral Survey Process

01

Document Review

We review your existing land documents, title deeds, and any historical survey records to understand the property's legal context and current documentation status.

02

Site Inspection

Our surveyors visit the property to identify existing boundary markers, assess site conditions, and locate reference points for accurate boundary determination.

03

Boundary Measurement

Using GNSS RTK receivers and total stations, we precisely measure and establish all boundary corner points with permanent markers and coordinates.

04

Neighbor Notification

Adjacent property owners are notified of the boundary survey to ensure transparency and provide an opportunity to confirm or dispute boundary locations.

05

Plan Preparation

Cadastral plans are prepared according to Ministry of Lands standards, including accurate parcel dimensions, areas, boundary descriptions, and coordinate data.

06

Official Submission

We submit the certified cadastral survey documentation to the Ministry of Lands for official registration and title deed processing on your behalf.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a cadastral survey?

A cadastral survey is a detailed land survey that establishes and documents legal property boundaries. It defines the exact location, shape, and area of a parcel of land, creating official records used for property ownership, land registration, and legal documentation.

Why do I need a cadastral survey in Zanzibar?

A cadastral survey is essential for property registration, land title applications, boundary dispute resolution, property sales, and land subdivision. In Zanzibar, the Ministry of Lands requires certified cadastral surveys for official land registration and title deed processing.

How long does a cadastral survey take in Zanzibar?

The timeline depends on the complexity and size of the property. A standard residential plot typically takes 3-5 working days including field work and documentation. Larger parcels or properties with boundary complexities may take 7-14 working days.

How much does a cadastral survey cost in Zanzibar?

Cadastral survey costs in Zanzibar vary based on plot size, location accessibility, boundary complexity, and documentation requirements. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your specific property and requirements.

What documents do I need for a cadastral survey?

Typically, you'll need proof of land ownership (such as a title deed, certificate of occupancy, or allocation letter), identification documents, and any existing survey plans. Our team will guide you through the specific requirements during your consultation.

What is the difference between a cadastral survey and a boundary survey?

While often used interchangeably, a cadastral survey is specifically designed for legal land registration and title purposes, creating official records accepted by government authorities. A boundary survey may be for general boundary identification. Our cadastral surveys meet all Ministry of Lands requirements.

Can you help with boundary disputes in Zanzibar?

Yes, we provide expert cadastral survey services for boundary disputes. Our licensed surveyors conduct precise measurements, review historical records, and provide legally documented boundary determinations that can be used in mediation or legal proceedings.

Are your cadastral surveys accepted by the Zanzibar government?

Absolutely. All our cadastral surveys are conducted by licensed surveyors and comply with the Land Survey Act and Ministry of Lands requirements in Zanzibar. Our surveys are officially recognized for land registration, title deed applications, and all government purposes.

What deliverables do I receive from a cadastral survey?

Deliverables include official cadastral plans, deed plans, title maps, coordinate data, boundary descriptions, area calculations, and survey reports. All documents are prepared in formats required by the Ministry of Lands and come with our licensed surveyor's certification.

Do you provide cadastral surveys for land subdivision in Zanzibar?

Yes, we provide comprehensive cadastral survey services for land subdivision, including creating new parcel boundaries, preparing subdivision plans, and coordinating with the Ministry of Lands for official registration of new plots.
