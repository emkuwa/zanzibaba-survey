Our Process

Our Boundary Survey Process

01

Document Review

We examine your title deed (Hatamilki), previous survey plans, and any relevant legal documents to understand the claimed boundaries and historical context of your property.

02

Record Research

Our team researches official records at the Ministry of Lands and Human Settlements Development to identify original survey data, boundary descriptions, and any recorded adjustments.

03

Field Investigation

We locate and examine physical evidence including existing boundary monuments, walls, fences, and natural features that may indicate boundary positions.

04

Boundary Computation

Using precise measurements and historical data, we compute the exact positions of all property corners and boundary lines according to official records.

05

Corner Marking

We physically mark all property corners with permanent concrete monuments containing stainless steel markers and GPS coordinates for future reference.

06

Documentation & Certification

You receive comprehensive boundary survey documentation including official plans, GPS coordinate reports, and a certified surveyor's report suitable for legal and registration purposes.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a boundary survey?

A boundary survey is a detailed investigation that establishes the exact legal boundaries of a property. It involves examining title deeds, historical records, and physical evidence to identify and mark the precise corners and lines that define property ownership.

Why do I need a boundary survey in Zanzibar?

Boundary surveys are essential for resolving property disputes, preventing encroachments, confirming property lines before construction, supporting land transactions, and meeting legal requirements for land registration with the Ministry of Lands in Zanzibar.

How much does a boundary survey cost in Zanzibar?

Boundary survey costs in Zanzibar depend on property size, complexity, and whether there are existing disputes. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your specific property requirements.

How long does a boundary survey take?

A standard boundary survey for a residential plot typically takes 1-3 days. Complex properties with disputed boundaries or extensive research requirements may take 5-10 days. We provide time estimates after initial assessment.

What documents do I need for a boundary survey?

You should provide your title deed (Hatamilki), any previous survey plans, neighboring property information, and any relevant court orders or agreements. We can also assist with obtaining records from the Ministry of Lands.

What is the difference between a boundary survey and a cadastral survey?

A boundary survey specifically identifies and marks property boundaries, often for dispute resolution or confirmation. A cadastral survey is a comprehensive mapping of all property boundaries in an area, typically used for land registration and tax purposes.

Can a boundary survey resolve property disputes?

Yes, boundary surveys are the primary tool for resolving property disputes in Zanzibar. Our surveys provide legally recognized documentation that can be presented to the Ministry of Lands, courts, or mediation committees to settle boundary disagreements.

What happens if my neighbor doesn't agree with the boundary survey?

If a neighbor disputes the survey results, the Ministry of Lands can appoint an independent surveyor to verify the findings. Our surveys are based on official records and are legally defensible, providing strong evidence in any boundary dispute.

Do boundary surveys include GPS coordinates?

Yes, all our boundary surveys include precise GPS coordinates for each property corner using RTK GNSS technology. These coordinates are referenced to the Tanzania National Coordinate System and can be used for future surveys and legal documentation.
