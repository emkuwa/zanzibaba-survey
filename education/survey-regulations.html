Survey Regulations in Zanzibar

Understanding the legal framework governing surveying in Tanzania

The Legal Framework

Surveying in Zanzibar is governed by a comprehensive legal framework that ensures accuracy, professionalism, and public trust in survey outcomes. The key legislation includes the Land Act No. 4 of 2010, the Land Zanzibar Act, and the Surveyors Act, which together establish the standards and requirements for surveying practice in the archipelago.

Licensed Surveyors

Only licensed surveyors are authorized to conduct land surveys in Zanzibar. The Zanzibar Board of Surveyors oversees the licensing process, ensuring that practitioners meet rigorous educational, experience, and ethical standards. Licensed surveyors bear personal liability for the accuracy of their work, which is registered with the Ministry of Lands.

Zanzibaba Survey employs fully licensed surveyors registered with both the Zanzibar and Tanzania mainland boards, ensuring our work meets all regulatory requirements.

Survey Standards

  • Accuracy Standards: All surveys must meet minimum accuracy standards defined by the Surveyors Act and relevant technical specifications.
  • Coordinate System: Surveys must use the Tanzania coordinate system (ARC 1960 / UTM Zone 37S for Zanzibar) for consistency with national datasets.
  • Documentation: Complete survey documentation, including field notes, calculations, and plans, must be maintained and submitted to the Land Registry.
  • Ethical Requirements: Surveyors must maintain independence, avoid conflicts of interest, and act in the public interest at all times.

Why Compliance Matters

Surveys conducted by unlicensed individuals or that fail to meet regulatory standards may be rejected by the Ministry of Lands, causing delays and additional costs.
