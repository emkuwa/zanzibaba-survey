Understanding Title Deeds in Zanzibar

Your guide to land title types, registration, and ownership in Zanzibar

Types of Title Deeds in Zanzibar

Zanzibar's land tenure system includes several types of title deeds, each with different legal implications and ownership rights. Understanding these distinctions is crucial for anyone buying, selling, or developing property in the archipelago.

  • Right of Occupancy (General): The most common form of land title, granting the holder exclusive rights to use and develop the land for a specified period, typically 33 or 99 years.
  • Right of Occupancy (Granted): Issued by the government for specific purposes such as commercial or industrial development, with defined conditions and lease terms.
  • Freehold Title: Available only to Tanzanian citizens, granting permanent ownership of the land without time limitation.
  • Customary Rights: Traditional land rights recognized under local customs, particularly in rural areas of Pemba and outer islands.

The Registration Process

Land registration in Zanzibar is managed through the Ministry of Lands and the Zanzibar Land Management Information System (ZLMIS). The process involves an application for registration, verification of ownership through a survey, payment of applicable fees, and issuance of a Certificate of Title. A professional survey is a mandatory step in this process, as it establishes the precise boundaries and area of the property.

Transfer and Inheritance

Title deed transfer in Zanzibar requires a Deed of Transfer executed before a licensed advocate, payment of stamp duty, and registration with the Land Registry. Inheritance cases follow similar procedures under the Law of Marriage Act and the Wills, Probate and Administration of Estates Act.
