Land Investment Guide for Zanzibar

Your complete guide to buying and investing in land in Zanzibar

Why Invest in Zanzibar Land?

Zanzibar's real estate market has experienced significant growth, driven by tourism development, infrastructure investment, and the islands' increasing appeal as a destination for both leisure and business. Land in prime locations such as Nungwi, Kendwa, Paje, and the Stone Town periphery has shown consistent appreciation, making it an attractive investment opportunity.

Key Considerations for Land Buyers

  • Title Verification: Always verify the title deed through the Ministry of Lands. Ensure the seller has legal authority to sell and there are no encumbrances or disputes.
  • Survey Verification: Commission an independent survey to confirm plot boundaries, area, and topography before purchase.
  • Zoning Regulations: Check the land-use zoning to understand what development is permitted on the plot.
  • Access and Infrastructure: Verify road access, water supply, electricity availability, and drainage infrastructure.
  • Environmental Considerations: Be aware of environmental protection zones, setback requirements from the coastline, and any environmental impact assessment requirements.

The Due Diligence Process

A thorough due diligence process is essential before any land purchase in Zanzibar. This should include a title search at the Land Registry, a boundary survey to verify the plot matches the deed, a topographical assessment for development feasibility, and legal review by a qualified Tanzanian property lawyer. Zanzibaba Survey can assist with the surveying components of this process, providing you with accurate, independent data to support your investment decision.

Foreign Ownership Rules

Foreign nationals can own land in Zanzibar through leasehold arrangements (typically 33 or 99 years) rather than freehold ownership. It is important to understand the terms and conditions of land leases and the process for renewal before making an investment.
