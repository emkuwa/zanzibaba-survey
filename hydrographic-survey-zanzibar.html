Our Process

Our Hydrographic Survey Process

01

Project Planning & Permitting

We assess project requirements, obtain necessary marine permits, plan survey lines and coverage, and coordinate with local authorities and port operations.

02

Tide & Water Level Monitoring

Establish tide gauges and monitor water level variations throughout the survey period. This data is essential for reducing depth measurements to chart datum.

03

Positioning & Calibration

Set up differential GNSS reference stations and calibrate sonar equipment. System patches and tests ensure instrument accuracy and reliability.

04

Data Acquisition

Systematic survey lines are run using multibeam or single-beam echo sounders. Side-scan sonar captures seabed imagery for bottom classification.

05

Data Processing & Analysis

Raw data is processed, filtered, and corrected for tides, vessel motion, and sound velocity. Digital terrain models and contour maps are generated.

06

Quality Assurance & Delivery

Rigorous quality checks verify accuracy against IHO standards. You receive comprehensive deliverables including charts, reports, and digital data files.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hydrographic survey?

A hydrographic survey is the science of measurement and description of features which affect maritime navigation, marine construction, dredging, offshore resource exploration, and related activities. It involves measuring water depths, mapping the seabed topography, and identifying underwater features and hazards.

What is the difference between hydrographic and bathymetric surveys?

While often used interchangeably, hydrographic surveys are broader in scope and include water depth measurements, seabed characterization, shoreline mapping, and tidal observations. Bathymetric surveys specifically focus on measuring water depth and mapping the underwater terrain to create detailed seabed topography maps.

How long does a hydrographic survey take in Zanzibar?

Duration depends on the survey area size, water conditions, and required detail. A small harbor survey may take 2-3 days, while a large-scale coastal mapping project could take 2-4 weeks. We provide detailed timelines after initial site assessment.

What equipment do you use for hydrographic surveys?

We use professional-grade multibeam echo sounders, single-beam echo sounders, side-scan sonar, GNSS positioning systems, and hydrographic processing software. All equipment meets IHO (International Hydrographic Organization) standards for accuracy.

Do I need a hydrographic survey for port construction in Zanzibar?

Yes, hydrographic surveys are mandatory for port and harbor construction projects. They provide essential seabed data for design, dredging planning, navigation channel layout, and ensuring safe vessel operations.

What deliverables do I receive from a hydrographic survey?

Deliverables typically include bathymetric charts, seabed topography maps, cross-sections, digital terrain models, nautical charts, and comprehensive survey reports with tide and water level data.

Can you perform hydrographic surveys in coral reef areas?

Yes, we have extensive experience conducting hydrographic surveys in coral reef environments around Zanzibar. We use specialized techniques and equipment to safely map seabed features while minimizing environmental impact on sensitive marine ecosystems.

What accuracy standards do your hydrographic surveys meet?

Our hydrographic surveys meet or exceed IHO Order 1a and Order 2 standards. We achieve vertical accuracy of ±10-30cm depending on water depth, and horizontal accuracy of ±1-5m using differential GNSS positioning.

Do you provide hydrographic surveys for environmental impact assessments?

Yes, we provide hydrographic data for environmental impact assessments (EIA) including baseline seabed mapping, sedimentation studies, marine habitat surveys, and post-construction monitoring for coastal development projects.
