Our Process

Our Drone Survey Process

01

Mission Planning

We plan the flight mission including area definition, flight altitude, overlap parameters, and ground control point locations based on your project requirements.

02

Ground Control Setup

Survey-grade ground control points (GCPs) are established using RTK GNSS to ensure survey-grade accuracy in the final deliverables.

03

Aerial Data Collection

Our licensed pilots execute the planned flight mission, capturing overlapping aerial photographs and sensor data according to photogrammetric best practices.

04

Photogrammetric Processing

Raw imagery is processed using advanced photogrammetry software to generate orthomosaics, point clouds, and 3D models with high positional accuracy.

05

Quality Assurance

Rigorous quality checks verify accuracy against ground control points, check completeness of coverage, and validate all derived products.

06

Deliverable Preparation

Final deliverables are prepared in multiple formats including GeoTIFF, SHP, DXF, LAS, and PDF. All products include a comprehensive survey report.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a drone survey?

A drone survey uses unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) equipped with high-resolution cameras and sensors to capture aerial imagery and geospatial data. This data is processed using photogrammetry software to create accurate maps, 3D models, orthomosaics, and digital elevation models for various applications.

How accurate are drone surveys in Zanzibar?

Our drone surveys achieve 2-5cm ground sampling distance (GSD) accuracy with proper ground control points. For most mapping applications, we achieve horizontal accuracy of ±3cm and vertical accuracy of ±5cm. Larger area surveys may have slightly lower accuracy depending on flight altitude and conditions.

How long does a drone survey take?

Flight time depends on the area size and mission parameters. A typical 50-hectare survey takes 1-2 hours of flight time. Processing usually takes 24-48 hours. Total turnaround from field work to final deliverables is typically 3-5 business days.

What industries use drone surveys in Zanzibar?

Drone surveys are used across multiple industries in Zanzibar including real estate development, construction monitoring, agriculture, coastal management, tourism infrastructure, environmental conservation, mining/quarrying, and urban planning.

Do you need a license to fly drones in Zanzibar?

Yes, commercial drone operations in Tanzania require authorization from the Tanzania Civil Aviation Authority (TCAA). Zanzibaba Survey holds all necessary permits and licenses for commercial drone survey operations throughout Zanzibar and Tanzania.

What deliverables do I receive from a drone survey?

Deliverables include orthomosaic maps, digital elevation models (DEM), digital surface models (DSM), 3D point clouds, 3D textured mesh models, contour maps, volume calculations, and georeferenced aerial photographs in standard formats (GeoTIFF, SHP, DXF, LAS/LAZ).

Can drones survey coastal areas and beaches?

Yes, drone surveys are ideal for coastal monitoring in Zanzibar. We capture shoreline changes, beach erosion patterns, coastal vegetation mapping, and tidal zone analysis. Drones can safely access areas that are difficult to reach by land survey methods.

How much does a drone survey cost in Zanzibar?

Drone survey costs depend on the area size, required accuracy, and deliverable complexity. We offer competitive rates for projects of all sizes. Contact us for a free quote based on your specific requirements.

What is the maximum area a drone can survey?

Our professional survey drones can cover up to 200 hectares per flight mission. For larger areas, we conduct multiple overlapping flights and stitch the results together. There is effectively no upper limit to the area we can survey.

Can drone surveys be done on cloudy days?

Drone surveys can be conducted in overcast conditions, but direct sunlight produces better imagery quality. We avoid flying in rain, high winds (>35 km/h), or low visibility conditions. Zanzibar's tropical climate provides many suitable flying days throughout the year.
